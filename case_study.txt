1. Synopsis 
ConnectSphere is a full-stack Social Media Mini Platform that enables users to share posts, engage 
through likes and comments, follow other users, discover trending content, and communicate via 
stories. The platform supports three primary roles — Guests (unauthenticated visitors), Registered 
Users, and Administrators — each with a purpose-built dashboard and a distinct set of capabilities. 
 
Registered users can create text and media posts with configurable visibility, interact with content 
through reactions and threaded comments, follow and unfollow other users, manage their notification 
feed, and upload stories with a 24-hour expiry. A real-time hashtag indexing and search service allows 
users to discover trending topics and find posts or people across the platform. 
 
Administrators can moderate all content, manage user accounts, review flagged reports, view 
platform-wide analytics, and send global notifications. ConnectSphere is built on a microservices 
architecture — modelled on the EShoppingZone reference pattern — with independent services for 
authentication/user management, posts, comments, likes/reactions, follow relationships, notifications, 
media/stories, and search/hashtags, all integrated through a Spring MVC website controller layer.

2.1 General Requirements 
•​
Guests can browse public posts, view public profiles, and search users or posts without logging 
in. 
•​
Users must register or log in (email/password or OAuth) to create posts, comment, like, or 
follow. 
•​
Role-based access control: Guest, Registered User, and Admin with distinct permission sets. 
•​
All users can update their profile picture, bio, username, and email at any time. 
•​
In-app and email notifications are dispatched for all key social events. 
•​
Admin panel provides full platform management, content moderation, and analytics access. 
 
2.2 Guest Requirements 
•​
Browse the public post feed without authentication. 
•​
View public user profiles including their posts, follower count, and bio. 
•​
Search for users by username or full name. 
•​
Search for posts by keyword or hashtag. 
•​
View trending hashtags and the posts associated with them. 
•​
Access the registration and login pages.

4.3 Comment-Service

to a postId, while replies carry a parentCommentId forming a two-level thread. The service maintains a 
per-comment likes counter and provides soft-delete so moderation history is preserved. Every comment 
or reply addition triggers a Post-Service counter increment via inter-service REST call.

4.7 Media / Story-Service

incremented atomically. The service supports soft-delete of media to preserve audit trails after post 
deletion.

computes trending hashtags by post-count ranking and provides full-text search across posts and 
users.

8. Glossary 
Term 
Definition 
User 
A registered member of ConnectSphere who can create and interact with 
content 
Post 
A unit of user-generated content consisting of text and/or attached media 
with a visibility setting 
Comment 
A user response attached to a post; may be top-level or a reply to another 
comment 
Reaction 
A typed like (Like, Love, Haha, Wow, Sad, Angry) applied to a post or 
comment by a user 
Follow 
A directed relationship where a follower subscribes to a followee's post 
feed 
Story 
An ephemeral media post visible to followers that automatically expires 
after 24 hours 
Hashtag 
A #keyword token embedded in post content used to categorise and 
discover related posts 
Trending 
A hashtag or topic ranked by highest post-count within a recent time 
window 
Visibility 
The audience scope of a post: Public (anyone), Followers-only, or Private 
(author only) 
Notification 
An in-app or email alert triggered by a social event (like, comment, follow, 
mention) 
Feed 
A personalised, chronologically ordered stream of posts from a user's 
followees 
Mention 
A @username reference in a post or comment that notifies the tagged user 
Reaction Summary 
An aggregated map of reaction types to counts displayed as an emoji bar 
on posts 
Microservice 
An independently deployable service responsible for a single, well-defined 
business domain 
JWT 
JSON Web Token — a compact, URL-safe token for stateless 
authentication across services 
CDN 
Content Delivery Network — a geographically distributed system for fast 
media file delivery
